GenerTTS: Pronunciation Disentanglement for Timbre and Style Generalization in Cross-Lingual Text-to-Speech

Abstract

This paper focuses on disentangling the components of speech, including timbre, style, and language, and enabling the free combination of these attributes in multilingual scenarios. Two primary challenges in achieving this objective are identified: cross-lingual style transfer and cross-lingual timbre transfer. To tackle these challenges, we present Speech-Weaver, a transfer- able multi-lingual multi-style, and multi-timbre text-to-speech system. The system can generate speech with a specified tim- bre and style, regardless of the spoken language, and without the need for parallel datasets. Specifically, we first separate timbre and other features by using a self-supervised speech representation as the output target of the encoder. Second, a style adaptor is proposed to extract fine-grained style infor- mation and reduce non-native accents in style based on mu- tual information minimization. Experiment results show that Speech-Weaver outperforms baseline systems in terms of natu- ralness and pronunciation.
